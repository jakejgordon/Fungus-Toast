# Temporary Mold Sprite Prompts

This file contains many of the ChatGPT prompts that were used to generate the mold image sprites that are in use today.

## Caveats
- Some of these images required additional manipulation post-prompt to tweak them to their final state
- These prompts routinely failed to result in images that had a transparent background. ChatGPT typically created a checkered background instead of truly making it transparent.
- The final image results that are in place now may be a better indication of the desired outcome, but these prompts may still be useful.

## Orange Dense Alt
Create a single top-down semi-realistic sprite of an alternate dense mature orange-red bread mold colony for a strategy game tile. Transparent background. This image will be resized to 64x64 pixels, so strong patch silhouette, readable internal massing, and edge variation matter more than tiny texture detail. Use the provided isolated orange-red anchor image as the canonical species reference. This image must be the same species as that approved isolated anchor: a shaggy woolly mold colony with soft aerial hyphae, fuzzy hairlike filaments, warm rusty apricot-orange coloration, pale cream fuzzy fringe, and a denser ember-colored core. Preserve that species identity and color family, but make this alternate dense-growth version a different fused expression of the same organism. This sprite is specifically an alternate dense expression for breaking up repetition in large mature patches. It should feel like the same species as the main dense tile, but with a different fused perimeter rhythm, different clump arrangement, and different placement of darker ember or cinnamon pockets. Keep the hairy aerial texture, but avoid a centered burst or overly symmetrical bloom. The silhouette should feel looser, messier, and more irregular than a pom-pom. Use the same warm palette family as the anchor: rusty orange, apricot-orange, muted ember red, cinnamon-brown shadowing, and pale cream to ivory fuzzy strands. Keep it clearly orange-red overall and distinct from the existing red mold. Avoid crimson rosette reads, neon orange, pink, gold, or muddy brown. Generate as a PNG with a real alpha channel. The background must be fully transparent alpha, not a checkerboard transparency preview. Do not render gray-and-white checker squares. Only the mold pixels should be opaque; all non-mold pixels must have 0% opacity. The colony should occupy roughly 76 percent to 88 percent of the canvas, with about 2 to 4 px of transparent margin on most sides. Keep it fully inside the frame, but make the spacing to the border uneven and asymmetrical. Preserve several readable off-center darker masses so neighboring dense tiles do not form a repeated fluffy burst pattern. Do not turn this into a flower, rosette, firework burst, fur pom-pom, coral fan, or decorative medallion. Keep it as a dense hairy, fuzzy, woolly orange-red mold colony with soft aerial hyphae and uneven off-center darker core pockets. Top-down only. No perspective. No cast shadow. No glow, halo, bloom, hard black outline, bread, environment, text, symbols, or pixels touching the border.
